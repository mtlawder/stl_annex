Marathon's are getting Faster

(and it's not just the shoes)


Times always get faster. Go to any major race and look at times five, ten, or even 20 years ago compare times to the results today. Yes, there are years when race conditions are unfavorable and runners struggle. But if you take the long look (and if anyone knows how to take the long look, it's marathoners), the times get faster. Shoes get a little (or more recently a lot) better, training becomes more focused, more and better athletes join the race and times get faster. Running may be better than most sports at adhering to the adage that "records (especially, personal records) were meant to be broken". I took a look at several major marathons to get a more numerical take of what just how these races are seeing more fast results. I'm not going to break down why people are running faster. You can go visit Let's Run or your favorite other message board to argue about that. Instead I give a statistical breakdown of the following Marathon's and how these races times have changed.
